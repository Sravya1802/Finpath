Do I need a Social Security Number (SSN) to build credit? â–¼

Yes, an SSN is typically required to build credit in the U.S. However, some financial institutions may accept an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) for certain services. F-1 students can apply for an SSN if they have on-campus employment or OPT authorization.

How do I file taxes as a non-resident? â–¼

F-1 students typically file Form 1040NR or 1040NR-EZ if they have U.S. income. H-1B workers are usually considered resident aliens for tax purposes and file Form 1040. The filing deadline is April 15th. Consider consulting a tax professional familiar with non-resident tax rules.

What's the best way to send money internationally? â–¼

Compare options like Wise (formerly TransferWise), Remitly, or traditional banks. Consider fees (usually 1-3% of amount), exchange rates, and transfer speed. Many services offer competitive rates and lower fees than traditional banks.

Can I open a bank account without an SSN? â–¼

Some banks may allow you to open an account with a passport and visa, but an SSN or ITIN is often required. Many banks offer special accounts for international students. Check with local banks or credit unions about their specific requirements.
